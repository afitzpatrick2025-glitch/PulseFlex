The science

What is sEMG, and why does it matter?

PulseFlex is built around the idea that better muscle data can lead to smarter training decisions and more informed recovery.

Technical explanation

Surface electromyography records the electrical activity generated by skeletal muscles during contraction. These signals can be collected noninvasively from the skin using electrodes placed over the target muscle group.

In PulseFlex, the patch concept is designed to sit directly above the muscle so the sensor can detect activation patterns during training and repeated movement. Changes in signal amplitude, timing, and consistency may help reflect how strongly a muscle is activating and how that activation changes across a session.

Over time, those signal patterns could be used to estimate fatigue-related trends, asymmetry between sides, or unusually high workload in specific muscle groups. That makes sEMG especially compelling for sports performance, rehabilitation, and overuse prevention.

Simple explanation for customers

Every time a muscle works, it sends out tiny electrical signals.

PulseFlex is designed to read those signals from the surface of the skin using a patch placed right over the muscle you want to track.

That means instead of only seeing broad information like heart rate or steps, users can get a more specific view of how hard a muscle is working and when that muscle may be getting tired.

In simple terms: PulseFlex helps turn muscle activity into information you can actually use.

Why it is more specific

Because the patch sits over a target muscle, it is designed to collect localized muscle data instead of relying on general wrist-based movement signals.

Why athletes care

More specific muscle feedback could help athletes understand workload, improve recovery planning, and notice patterns linked to overuse.

Why the app matters

Raw electrical signals are not useful to most people on their own, so the app turns them into simple, readable insights for daily decision-making.

Why we made it

Created by three student-athletes who wanted answers they could not find anywhere else.

PulseFlex was created from a real problem. As founders Ella Brophy, Kirsten Clarius, and Ailish Fitzpatrick, and as members of the Florida Institute of Technology women’s lacrosse team, we have spent countless hours training, competing, and recovering while feeling like we still lacked access to specific information about what our muscles were actually going through.

We wanted more than generalized fitness data. We wanted a way to understand muscle fatigue earlier, track workload more precisely, and reduce the kind of repeated overuse that can quietly build into bigger injuries over time.

That is where PulseFlex started: not as a gadget for the sake of technology, but as a tool designed around a need we personally felt as athletes. We wanted something more targeted, more wearable, and more useful for real performance decisions.

FAQ

Questions customers will ask before buying

These answers help explain the product clearly while keeping the language approachable and specific.

What does PulseFlex actually measure?

PulseFlex is designed to measure surface electromyography, or sEMG. That means it reads the electrical activity produced by muscles during contraction from the skin surface.

What is sEMG in simple terms?

sEMG is a way of listening to how hard a muscle is working. When a muscle contracts, it gives off small electrical signals. PulseFlex is designed to pick up those signals and make them easier to understand.

Why use a patch instead of a watch?

A patch can be placed directly over a specific muscle group, which makes it more useful for targeted muscle monitoring than a general wrist-worn device.

How much does PulseFlex cost?

The PulseFlex patch is priced at about $149, and the mobile app operates on a subscription model of around $9.99 per month.
